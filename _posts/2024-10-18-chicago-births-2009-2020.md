## Sources

Detailed data on babies born to Chicago residents come from a number of sources:

- The Illinois Department of Public Health
  [publishes demographic details on babies born to Chicago residents](https://dph.illinois.gov/data-statistics/vital-statistics/birth-statistics/more-statistics.html)
  by year from 2009 through 2021.
- The
  [Chicago Department of Public Health published data on total births from 1999-2008](https://data.cityofchicago.org/api/assets/8C4E8E51-6162-4DF3-9C29-D3F205FA2FB4).
- Births by race and ethnicity from 2009-2014 were
  [received from a FOIA request](https://www.muckrock.com/foi/springfield-11335/chicago-births-2010-2021-130439/).
- [CDPH, Births in Chicago, 1999-2009](https://www.chicago.gov/content/dam/city/depts/cdph/statistics_and_reports/BirthsinChicago1999thru2009Dec172012.pdf)
- Birth data from 1995-2004 was published in a
  [2007 Chicago Department of Health Report](https://www.chicago.gov/content/dam/city/depts/cdph/statistics_and_reports/SR_ChgoBOIMrpt07.pdf)
- [IDPH, Births by County of Residence, 1990-1999](http://www.idph.state.il.us/health/bdmd/birth1.htm)
- [Illinois Data Portal, All Live Births In Illinois, 1989-2009](https://data.illinois.gov/dataset/77all_live_births_in_illinois_19892009/resource/d7ec861b-6b7c-4260-82d8-3f05f49053f9)
- [birth outcomes and infant mortality in Chicago, 2006](https://www.chicago.gov/dam/city/depts/cdph/statistics_and_reports/SR_ChgoBOIMrpt05.pdf)
- [Total Births by Race in Chicago](https://datacenter.kidscount.org/data/tables/11373-total-births-by-race?loc=1&loct=3#detailed/3/103/false/36,868,867,6,5,4,3,2,1/3,2,1,4,13/22014,22015)

I've
[compiled this data into a spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/11puU8gupkp0gjzN_LXMQaSYbhYoJLr5aylaclxhawDw/edit#gid=0).

## Total Fertility Rate

> [Total Fertility Rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_fertility_rate) is
> the average number of children that would be born to a woman over her lifetime
> if:

> - she were to experience the exact current age-specific fertility rates
>   (ASFRs) through her lifetime
> - she were to live from birth until the end of her reproductive life.

The replacement total fertility rate is about 2.1

The Chicago total fertility rate has been below 2.1 since 2001, according to
data from the
[Chicago Department of Public Health.](https://chicagohealthatlas.org/indicators/VRTFER?topic=total-fertility-rate)
and
[other](https://dph.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/idph/files/publications/population-projections-report-final-2014-041516.pdf)
[sources](https://www.chicago.gov/content/dam/city/depts/cdph/statistics_and_reports/BirthsinChicago1999thru2009Dec172012.pdf).

```js

```

The total fertility rate for Hispanics and African Americans fell below

```js
const total_fertility_rate = total_fertility_rate_raw
  .map((d) => [
    { year: d.Category, race: "total", rate: d["Full population"] },
    { year: d.Category, race: "Hispanic", rate: d["Hispanic or Latino"] },
    { year: d.Category, race: "Asian", rate: d["Asian or Pacific Islander"] },
    {
      year: d.Category,
      race: "African American",
      rate: d["Non-Hispanic Black"],
    },
    { year: d.Category, race: "white", rate: d["Non-Hispanic White"] },
  ])
  .flat()
  .map(({ year, rate, ...d }) => ({
    year: new Date(year.toString()),
    rate: parseFloat(rate) / 1000,
    ...d,
  }))
  .filter((d) => d.year.getTime() !== new Date("2017-01-01").getTime());
```
